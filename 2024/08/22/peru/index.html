        When I traveled, I collected brace lace for each country. Totally, I visited ten countries and collected ten brace laces. Among all of them, I preferred the one I bought in Peru most. Outside a small store along the street in Cusco, a brace lace with some geometric shapes, continuous triangles, attracted my attention. The sailor told me that means showing respect to mountains. As for a country like Peru, mountains could be found here and there. I believed in that ancestors of Peruvian must appreciated mountains for settlement and fear the disaster caused by them. As for me, I enjoyed traveling inside mountains and visiting mountainous spots, but slightly feared altitude sick.
        Leaving for Peru, I was excited again, for it was my first time in the south earth. It would be fun to obtain whirlpool becomes clockwise. Once I landed in Lima, I visited museums to learn its history. Larco Museum was one of my favorites, which demonstrated the whole Peruvian history and culture. Marvelous artworks from different periods made me feel like time traveling.

        Machu Pichu: Seven Wonders in the new world
        Our world is so mysterious that we cannot understand the basic rule as individuals. For me, I would like to feel it by observing and touching. It was just like my way to Machu Pichu. I have to say it was my love! From Lima, I took a fly to Cusco, a small town close to Machu Pichu. Every tourist had to passed by to visit this marvelous spot. I spent a few days in Cusco to get familiar with high altitude. Then, I took a bus to hot water town, which included 7-hour mountainous road trip and 3-hour hiking along tracks. That was exhausting without doubt. Fortunately, I made a lot of friends on the bus. They were all young people of my age from different countries, such as Argentina, Chile, Brazil and Israel. Thanks for them, my trip was not that tough. Although my legs and feet could hardly move, I still expected to Machu Pichu greatly. On the second day, I finally observed this wonder! Buildings (structures actually) stood on the top of mountains. I could not imagine how people from Inka Empire built them how they moved some many big stones from bottom to peaks. I also made a friend in Machu Pichu. She is Chinese working in Colombia, and she had traveled alone to most Latin America. At the end of my visit, some Peruvian kids asked me to take a photo together. I was happy not only because they asked for taking photos together, but also because I was at an opposite position. When I was young in China, kids were surprised at foreigners, and some may want to take a picture with them. Now, I became a foreigner for them. Another memorable story in Machu Pichu was on the ancient Inka Road. I met a group of old tourists lining up in a row from my opposite direction. I greeted to them and clapped hands with them one by one just like an athlete! I regrated not recording it, but the scene would always stay in my memory!

        Rainbow mountain was the highest place I have ever been. Over 5,000 meters made me hard to breath. The tour started at 4 am. Yes, 4 am! My tour guide drove a van, carrying a group of tourists with me. Two days ago, I got a severe sick on my stomachache, which made me fever and vomit at midnight. I was supposed to quit this tour, but I insisted it finally. After almost three hoursâ€™ drive, we arrived at the bottom of rainbow mountain. It is beautiful for sure! Dozens of colors belted on the surface of mountain. Behind it, it is the highest snow mountain in Peru. We stopped by 4,800 altitude, and the last 400 meters relied on ourselves. For me, altitude sickness and stomachache exhausted me. At the beginning, I could keep pace with the first group, but then I slowed down my speed and followed the majority slowly. My tour guide gave me a magic yellow liquid. Once I inhale it, my sickness disappeared at once. It might be a mixture of mint and something. I still did not figure it out so far. Step by step, I reached the peak finally. The view was so amazing! I appreciated this gift from the nature!
